# Negotiation: Negotiate & Trade

## Need
- Options on the table
- Parties ready to make trade-offs

## Instructions

### 1. Identify Trade-off Opportunities
1. What does Party A value that Party B can easily give?
2. What does Party B value that Party A can easily give?
3. Package multiple issues together for broader trade-offs

### 2. Propose & Counter
1. One party proposes a package
2. Other party responds: accept, counter, or propose alternative
3. Mediator reframes and facilitates movement

### 3. Use Objective Criteria
1. Reference external standards where possible: market rates, industry benchmarks, precedent, expert opinion, legal requirements
2. Separate people from the problem — focus on the issue

### 4. Manage Deadlock
| Technique | When |
|-----------|------|
| Pause/break | Emotions high |
| Ask "what if" | Stuck on a point |
| Change the variable | Adjust timeline, scope, resources |
| Bring in expert | Technical dispute |
| Escalate | Both parties need authority approval |
| BATNA check | Remind parties of the alternative to no deal |

## Done
- Trade-offs identified and exchanged
- Proposals and counter-proposals made
- Movement toward agreement
